Keystone Species

Beaver

Beavers use specialized teeth to chop down trees to then build dams.

With this, they shape the habitat and ensure good water quality and storage for the benefit of other organisms in the ecosystem.

Wolf

Wolves are pack animals that work together to secure food, water, and territory for the entire group.

Their ways of life serve the purpose of regulating the population of prey animals which in turn effects plant diversity and ecosystem productivity.

Prairie Dog

Prairie Dogs are burrowing ground squirrels that primary live in grasslands.

They act as ecosystem engineers with their burrows aerating soil and providing shelter to other organisms that come across an abandoned ones.

Sea Otter

Sea Otters are marine mammals the are native to the coasts of the northern and eastern Pacific Ocean.

They are a keystone species because they act as predators towards sea urchins that tend to over consume kelp which prevents them from destroying the kelp forest ecosystem.

Bee

Bees are eusocial insects that work together under a single queen to produce honey for the colony

Bees are also the poster-child for pollinating plant life and being the reason why approximately 30% of human foods are reliant on their labor.

Tiger Shark

Tiger Sharks are aquatic predator animals that can be found in tropical, warm, and coastal waters.

With their role, they ensure the health of the ecosystem by consuming sickly prey and protecting seagrass from turtle and dugongs.

Elephant

Elephants are the largest living land mammals on the planet.

They are considered ecosystem engineers because they dig up waterholes and spread the seed of plants around which creates a suitable habitat for other creatures.

Coral

Coral or Coral Polyps are tiny animals which in turn create Coral Reefs.

Corals Reefs are called biodiversity hotspots because they manage to create an entire habitat and food source for the benefit of all the organisms that live in it.

Hummingbird

Hummingbirds are a small bird species native to the Americas.

With their feeding habits, they also act as pollinators to spread the reach of the plants that they feed on.

Grizzly Bear

Grizzly Bears are a population of brown bear that are native to North America.

Their role as a keystone species is as prey regulation and the dispersal of seeds and nutrients by bring salmon carcasses into forests.

Saguaro Cactus

The Saguaro Cactus is the iconic plant of the Sonoran Desert of California and Arizona.

They offer a primary food, water, and shelter source for birds, bats, and other mammals.

Parrotfish

Parrotfish are vibrant and tropical fish known for the beak-like mouths.

As herbivores, they consume a lot of algae which enable new coral to grow in new places.
